Robert Mugabe, President of Zimbabwe

International Business Times, March 5, 2016



Robert Mugabe, born February 21, 1924, is the president of Zimbabwe. He was prime minister from 1980 to 1987, than president from then on. He was born into a poor family in Kuruma, South Rhodesia. He worked as a teacher in Rhodesia and Ghana until he joined African Protesters against the british colonies.He was imprisoned in 1964 until his release in 1974. He then fled to Mozambique, where his rise to political power began. He had a big role in eliminating white supremacy in Africa.
